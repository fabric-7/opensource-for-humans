# AI Safety Policy

AI can help OpenSource for Humans, but it must not replace human responsibility in sensitive decisions.

## Allowed Uses

- Classifying services by category.
- Summarizing public texts in plain language.
- Translating content.
- Detecting duplicates.
- Improving search.
- Suggesting triage questions.
- Supporting human reviewers.
- Identifying data that may be outdated.

## Prohibited Uses

- Saying that someone has a guaranteed right to a benefit.
- Providing medical diagnosis.
- Providing definitive legal advice.
- Prioritizing vulnerable people without transparent criteria.
- Publishing records automatically without human review.
- Responding to emergencies as a substitute for official services.
- Inferring sensitive data without necessity and proper legal basis.
- Using data from vulnerable people for training without authorization, legal basis, and risk review.

## Required Language

Recommendations must use honest uncertainty:

- "You may be eligible."
- "This service may apply to your situation."
- "Confirm with the responsible organization."
- "In an emergency, contact the official local service."

Avoid:

- "You have the right."
- "You will be served."
- "Guaranteed."
- "Approved."

## Human Review

Any AI that affects visibility, eligibility, safety, health, housing, legal support, or emergency support must have human review and a decision record.

## Transparency

When AI generates, summarizes, translates, or classifies content, that must be traceable for reviewers.

## Personal Data

Prompts, logs, and evaluations must not expose sensitive personal data unless strictly necessary.
