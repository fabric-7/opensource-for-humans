# Funding Policy

OpenSource for Humans needs to be sustainable without turning vulnerability into a product.

## Allowed

- Individual donations.
- Grants.
- Foundation support.
- University partnerships.
- Government contracts.
- Paid plans for larger organizations.
- Institutional ESG tools.
- Implementation consulting.
- Support, hosting, and training services.

## Prohibited

- Charging people who need to find help.
- Selling personal data.
- Selling priority placement in essential results.
- Accepting predatory advertising.
- Promoting abusive loans.
- Allowing false promises of benefits, jobs, medical outcomes, shelter, or money.
- Closing the core codebase because a funder requires it.
- Giving governance control to a sponsor.

## Separation Between Revenue and Trust

Payment must never be treated as a signal of social trustworthiness.

A paying organization may have better administrative tools, but it must not appear more trustworthy merely because it pays.

## Transparency

Relevant funders should be listed publicly.

Conflicts of interest must be disclosed.
