# Data Policy

This policy defines how OpenSource for Humans collects, reviews, publishes, corrects, and removes data about organizations and social support services.

## Core Principle

Data exists to help people find real support. If a data point increases risk without increasing usefulness, it should not be collected or published.

## Allowed Service Data

Institutional and public data may be published, such as:

- service name;
- responsible organization;
- description;
- category;
- city, neighborhood, and public service location;
- opening hours;
- official contacts;
- official website;
- cost;
- required documents;
- eligible or intended audience;
- languages;
- accessibility;
- source;
- status;
- last verification date.

## Prohibited Data About People Receiving Help

The following must not be published:

- tax IDs, national IDs, passports, or personal documents;
- residential addresses;
- personal phone numbers;
- individual health data;
- individual migration status;
- individual religion;
- identifiable personal stories;
- photos of people in vulnerable situations without proper authorization;
- data about children and adolescents, except when strictly necessary, aggregated, and protected.

## User Data Storage Rule

During the first stage of the project, personal data from people seeking help and general visitors must not be stored server-side.

The platform must not store user-level search history, precise location, eligibility answers, contact-click trails, session replay, or identifiable analytics for people seeking help.

If statistics are needed, they must be anonymized and aggregated before storage. Acceptable examples include total searches by category, total searches by city, total clicks by service, and total reports by issue type, as long as no person can be identified, singled out, tracked, or re-identified.

Organizational account data may be stored only when needed to operate verified organization profiles, and it must follow documented legal basis, limited purpose, strict access control, and retention limits.

## Sources

Accepted sources:

- official websites;
- government portals;
- institutional pages;
- direct contact with the organization;
- open data;
- confirmation by a trained volunteer;
- submission by the verified organization itself.

## Trust Levels

1. Suggested by the community.
2. Found in a public source.
3. Confirmed by a volunteer.
4. Managed by the organization.
5. Official partner.

## Review Before Publication

New services suggested by users, scraping, or AI must go through human review before appearing as verified.

## Updates

Each service must display its last verification date. Old services should enter a periodic review queue.

## Removal

A service must be removed or marked inactive when:

- it has ended;
- there is scam risk;
- the source is false;
- the organization requests a legitimate correction;
- data was improperly exposed;
- there is a serious violation of security or rights.

## Open Data

Institutional data may be made available through APIs or downloads when doing so does not increase risk for vulnerable people.

User personal data must not be published as open data.
