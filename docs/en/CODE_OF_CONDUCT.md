# Code of Conduct

OpenSource for Humans must be a safe, respectful, and useful community for people from different backgrounds, languages, countries, religions, identities, abilities, and social realities.

## Expected Behavior

- Treat people with respect.
- Assume good faith while taking real-world impact seriously.
- Use clear and welcoming language.
- Accept technical review and criticism without personal attacks.
- Protect vulnerable people.
- Avoid public exposure of personal data.
- Ask for help when a situation involves legal, social, or security risk.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, humiliation, or discrimination.
- Hate speech.
- Political, religious, commercial, or financial exploitation of vulnerable people.
- Publishing personal data without proper authorization and legal basis.
- False promises of benefits, jobs, medical outcomes, shelter, or money.
- Fraud, scams, or redirection to abusive services.
- Using the community for predatory self-promotion.
- Retaliation against people who report problems.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove content, block interactions, revoke permissions, or escalate cases to the community council.

Urgent situations involving people's safety must be handled with priority.
