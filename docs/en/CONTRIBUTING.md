# Contributing

Thank you for wanting to contribute to OpenSource for Humans.

This project accepts contributions in code, data, design, research, documentation, translation, accessibility, security, governance, and partnerships.

## Before Contributing

Read:

- [MANIFESTO.md](MANIFESTO.md)
- [CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md)
- [DATA_POLICY.md](DATA_POLICY.md)
- [PRIVACY_COMPLIANCE.md](PRIVACY_COMPLIANCE.md)
- [AI_SAFETY_POLICY.md](AI_SAFETY_POLICY.md)

## Essential Rules

1. Do not expose personal data from people in vulnerable situations.
2. Do not publish national IDs, identity documents, residential addresses, private phone numbers, or sensitive personal stories.
3. Do not promise benefits, appointments, jobs, shelter, medical outcomes, legal protection, or financial help.
4. Use simple, direct, accessible language.
5. Provide source and verification date when suggesting services.
6. Separate facts, estimates, and opinions.
7. Do not use AI to publish data without human review.
8. Prioritize accessibility and mobile use.
9. Respect local chapters and community knowledge.
10. When in doubt about privacy, choose not to collect, not to publish, or ask for review.

## Types of Contribution

## Code

Technical contributions should have clear scope, tests when applicable, and must not introduce unnecessary data collection.

## Data

Service data must include a public source or direct confirmation. Suggestions from the community must not be published automatically.

## Design

Interfaces should be simple, accessible, mobile-first, and appropriate for people with limited digital familiarity.

## Translation

Translations should preserve clarity and a welcoming tone. Avoid legalistic language when a simple sentence works.

## AI

AI features must follow [AI_SAFETY_POLICY.md](AI_SAFETY_POLICY.md).

## Pull Requests

A good pull request should include:

- what changed;
- why it changed;
- how it was tested;
- privacy, security, or social impact risks;
- screenshots for visual changes.

## Review

Maintainers may request changes for technical quality, privacy, security, accessibility, clarity, or alignment with the mission.
