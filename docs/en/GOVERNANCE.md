# Governance

This document defines how OpenSource for Humans makes decisions, distributes responsibilities, and protects its public-interest mission.

## Structure

## Community

Anyone can participate by proposing improvements, reviewing data, translating content, testing accessibility, reporting errors, or contributing code.

## Contributors

Contributors are people whose contributions are accepted by the community. They may work on technology, data, design, documentation, translation, research, security, partnerships, or moderation.

## Technical Maintainers

Technical maintainers review code, architecture, security, tests, deployments, and engineering quality.

## Data Maintainers

Data maintainers review sources, services, duplicates, status changes, reports, and verification logs.

## Community Council

Whenever possible, the community council should include people with experience in technology, social work, data protection, accessibility, security, social organizations, and directly affected communities.

The council decides high-impact matters, such as:

- mission changes;
- privacy policies;
- funding rules;
- AI use in sensitive flows;
- opening or restricting datasets;
- strategic partnerships;
- removing maintainers for serious violations.

## Local Chapters

Local chapters are city, regional, or country groups responsible for mapping, reviewing, and maintaining local data.

Each local chapter must follow the project's central documents, especially privacy, security, data, AI, and conduct policies.

## Decision Process

Small changes may be approved through a pull request reviewed by a maintainer.

Relevant changes should go through a public proposal, called an RFC.

Critical changes must be approved by the community council.

## RFCs

An RFC should explain:

- problem;
- proposal;
- impact on vulnerable people;
- privacy impact;
- technical impact;
- alternatives considered;
- rollback plan.

## Transparency

Relevant decisions should be publicly documented, unless doing so would expose personal data, operational security, or vulnerable people.

## Conflicts

Conflicts should be handled with respect, documentation, and mediation. Serious violations of the code of conduct may result in removal of permissions.
