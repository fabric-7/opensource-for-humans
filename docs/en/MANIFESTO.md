# Manifesto

OpenSource for Humans exists to make it simpler, safer, and more dignified to find social help.

Technology was built by people. When it is used in areas that shape essential parts of life, it must first serve human protection, autonomy, and dignity.

This project begins from the belief that trustworthy information about food, shelter, health, documents, employment, legal support, education, care, and protection should not be scattered, hidden, outdated, or accessible only to people who already know where to look.

## Mission

Build an open, free, and trustworthy platform that connects people in vulnerable situations with nearby social support services, bringing together governments, NGOs, faith communities, local groups, responsible companies, volunteers, and grassroots initiatives.

## Founding Principles

1. People seeking help are never the product.
2. Searching for help must work without mandatory login.
3. The project must collect the least personal data possible.
4. Sensitive data must receive the highest level of protection.
5. Published information must show its source, status, and last verification date.
6. AI must support people and reviewers, never replace human judgment in sensitive decisions.
7. No organization may buy priority placement in essential help results.
8. The community must be able to audit code, rules, decisions, and policies.
9. Local chapters must have autonomy to maintain their own data while respecting shared standards.
10. Financial sustainability is allowed; mission capture is not.

## Commitment

The goal is not to create only a directory. The goal is to build open social infrastructure so that anyone, anywhere, can discover real paths toward safety, care, and dignity.
