What the project is

OpenSource for Humans is open social infrastructure. The first version is a local, mobile-first MVP with anonymous search and a verified catalog of real services. The long-term vision is global, but the first step must be small, useful, and trustworthy.

For people seeking help

Find nearby services without creating an account, sharing an ID, providing an exact address, or having personal data stored server-side.

For organizations

Keep services up to date, respond to corrections, receive responsible visibility, and collaborate on a trustworthy public knowledge base.

For the community

Contribute code, data, review, design, translation, security, partnerships, outreach, and local chapters.

No login to find help No sale of personal data LGPD + GDPR/RGPD Verified data Human-reviewed AI

What we will build first

The MVP should prove that people can find real help through a simple experience. The recommended initial scope is one city, a few categories, and around 200 trustworthy services.

How to contribute

You do not need special permission to start. Pick a small piece, open an issue or pull request, explain the impact, and respect the privacy, data, and security policies.

Code and infrastructure

  • Frontend mobile-first.
  • Catalog API.
  • Admin panel.
  • Tests, CI, deployment, and observability.

Data and verification

  • Map real services.
  • Confirm official sources.
  • Review duplicates.
  • Fix hours, contacts, and status.

Design, docs, and community

  • Improve plain-language copy.
  • Test accessibility.
  • Translate documentation.
  • Help new contributors start.

First rule: if a change collects or stores personal data from people seeking help, stop and ask for review. At the initial stage, the product must work without storing that data server-side.

How to spread the word and bring more people in

An open source community grows when different people can find a concrete way to help. Outreach is not just posting links: it is building bridges, removing friction, and turning attention into contribution.

Outreach channels

  • Create project profiles on LinkedIn, Instagram, and YouTube.
  • Publish weekly progress: closed issues, reviewed data, and next calls for help.
  • Share specific calls: "we need to review 50 food support services".
  • Join technology communities, universities, NGOs, and local groups.
  • Create short presentations for events, meetups, and social hackathons.

Responsible sponsorships

  • Ask companies to support infrastructure, domains, events, design, and contributor grants.
  • Offer public recognition without selling priority in essential help results.
  • Create corporate volunteering campaigns for data review and accessibility.
  • Pursue grants, universities, foundations, and social impact programs.
  • Publish supporters and conflicts of interest transparently.

Initiative mindset

The project needs people who do what needs to be done responsibly. Do not wait for everything to be perfect. Pick a small part, document it, execute it, ask for review, and help the next person continue.

Notice a gap

Missing an issue, translation, contact, dataset, test, or outreach channel? Create the first draft.

Protect the mission

Growth cannot override privacy, security, human review, transparency, or the dignity of people seeking help.

Multiply people

The best contribution does not end with you. It leaves instructions, examples, and next steps for more people to join.
